SECTION 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE. This Employee Conduct and Operations Policy governs the day-to-day responsibilities of all staff, contractors, and temporary personnel engaged by the organization. It applies uniformly across every department, business unit, and regional office unless a written exception has been approved by the Office of Compliance. Nothing in this document supersedes applicable law; where a conflict arises, the stricter standard controls.

SECTION 2. WORKING HOURS. Standard working hours are defined as the interval between 09:00 and 17:30 local time, Monday through Friday, exclusive of a thirty-minute unpaid meal period. Flexible arrangements may be requested through the staffing portal and require approval from the reporting manager. Overtime must be authorized in advance and recorded against the appropriate cost center.

SECTION 3. EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT. Reimbursable expenses include travel undertaken for an approved business purpose, lodging at the standard nightly rate, and incidental costs not exceeding the published per-diem schedule. Receipts must be submitted within fourteen calendar days. Claims lacking adequate documentation will be returned for correction and may be denied if not resubmitted within the grace period.

SECTION 4. INFORMATION HANDLING. Personnel must classify every document they create as Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted, and apply the corresponding handling controls. Restricted material may not be transmitted over unmanaged channels or stored on personal devices. Access is granted on a least-privilege basis and reviewed quarterly by the data steward of record.

SECTION 5. LEAVE AND ABSENCE. Annual leave accrues monthly and may be carried forward up to the published ceiling. Sick leave requires notification to the reporting manager before the start of the scheduled shift. Extended absence beyond five consecutive working days requires supporting documentation filed with the Human Resources office.

SECTION 6. EQUIPMENT AND ASSETS. Issued equipment remains the property of the organization and must be returned in serviceable condition upon separation. Loss or damage must be reported within one business day. Software may be installed only from the approved catalog; unmanaged installations are subject to removal without notice.

SECTION 7. REVIEW AND AMENDMENT. This policy is reviewed annually by the Office of Compliance. Proposed amendments are circulated for comment for a period of fifteen working days before adoption. The version recorded in the central register is authoritative; locally cached copies are provided for convenience only and carry no independent force.
